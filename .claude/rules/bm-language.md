# Bahasa Malaysia Language Rules

All TikTok scripts generated by this pipeline must be in authentic Bahasa Malaysia with Malaysian slang. These rules apply to all script output, voiceover text, storyboard narrations, and UI text directed at the Malaysian audience.

## Core Requirement

**All scripts must be in Bahasa Malaysia.** The only acceptable English is:
- Product/brand names (e.g., "AeroGlow LED Face Mask", "Shopee")
- Prices in RM (e.g., "RM49.99", not "49.99 ringgit")
- Platform names (TikTok, Instagram)
- Hashtags (#skincare, #viral)
- Technical terms with no BM equivalent

## Malaysian Slang — Must Use

These expressions make the content sound authentic to Malaysian TikTok:

### Intensifiers & Affirmations
- `memang` — truly, genuinely ("Memang berbaloi beli!")
- `confirm` — definitely, for sure ("Confirm berjaya dalam 2 minggu")
- `power` — excellent, impressive ("Hasilnya power habis!")
- `mantap` — awesome, solid ("Mantap betul produk ni!")
- `gila` — extremely (casual intensifier, "Gila best hasilnya!")
- `best` — great, amazing ("Best gila rasa dia!")
- `berbaloi` — worth it ("Harga RM49 memang berbaloi!")

### Calls to Action
- `jom` — let's go / come on ("Jom grab sebelum habis!")
- `wajib` — must / obligatory ("Korang wajib cuba ni!")
- `grab` — get/buy (borrowed English, used naturally in BM TikTok)
- `rugi` — a waste / you'll miss out ("Rugi kalau tak ambil!")

### Personal Pronouns (conversational)
- `korang` — you all (informal plural, preferred over "anda" for TikTok)
- `kita` — we/us (inclusive)
- `saya` — I/me (slightly formal but acceptable)
- `dia` — it/he/she (also used for products — "Dia buat kulit korang...")

### Particles (essential for authentic BM)
- `lah` — softener, emphasis ("Cuba lah dulu")
- `tau` — you know, right? ("Best tau!")
- `kan` — isn't it? right? ("Mahal kan dekat spa?")
- `je` — only, just ("20 minit je sehari!")
- `ni` — this/here ("Produk ni memang viral")
- `tu` — that/there ("Cara guna dia tu mudah je")

### Social Proof Phrases
- `dah ramai yang cuba` — many have already tried
- `dah viral kat TikTok` — already viral on TikTok
- `lebih 50,000 orang` — more than 50,000 people
- `stok selalu habis` — stock always runs out
- `ramai yang reorder` — many people reorder
- `hasil dia memang nampak` — the results are visible

## Script Structure — BM Template

### Hook (5 seconds, ~20 words)
Must open with one of:
1. **Question:** "Korang tahu tak yang rawatan spa RM300 tu sebenarnya..."
2. **Shocking fact:** "RM49 je boleh buat benda sama macam klinik RM300"
3. **POV scenario:** "POV: Korang dah habis RM300 dekat spa tapi hasilnya..."
4. **Relatable problem:** "Kalau korang ada masalah kulit dan dah penat cuba..."

### Body (15 seconds, ~60 words)
Structure: **Masalah → Produk → Bukti → Kesan**

```
Masalah: [Sebutkan masalah yang relevan]
Produk: [Nama produk] selesaikan masalah tu dengan [cara/teknologi]
Bukti: Lebih [X] orang dah cuba dan [keputusan]
Kesan: Dalam masa [X] minggu/hari, [perubahan yang dijangka]
```

Example:
> "Rawatan red light therapy biasanya RM200 sesesi dekat spa. Tapi mask LED AeroGlow ni bawa teknologi sama ke rumah korang. 20 minit je, tiga kali seminggu — kulit korang akan berubah dalam masa 2 minggu. Kolagen meningkat, kulit makin cerah. Lebih 50,000 orang dah buktikan!"

### CTA (5 seconds, ~20 words)
Malaysian TikTok CTAs that convert:
```
✓ "Komen 'MAHU' dan saya DM link terus!"
✓ "Link ada kat bio — jom grab sebelum stok habis!"
✓ "Save video ni dulu, korang decide kemudian!"
✓ "Follow untuk tips macam ni setiap hari!"
✓ "Tag member yang wajib cuba produk ni!"
```

**Never use these English CTAs:**
- ❌ "Click the link in bio"
- ❌ "Don't miss out"
- ❌ "Limited time offer"
- ❌ "Shop now"
- ❌ "Comment below"

## Prices — Always in RM

- Use `RM` prefix (not "MYR", not "ringgit")
- Format: `RM49.90` (not `RM 49.90` or `RM49,90`)
- When comparing: "Dekat spa RM300, tapi ni RM49 je!"

## Storyboard Voiceover Text (per scene)

Each of the 6 scene voiceover texts must be:
- 1–2 sentences in BM
- Conversational tone (imagine talking to a kawan/friend)
- Naturally continue from the previous scene
- Reference the product naturally — not like a press release

**Scene 1–2 (Hook scenes):** Establish the problem or shocking fact
**Scene 3–4 (Body scenes):** Introduce product and results
**Scene 5 (Proof scene):** Social proof or before/after
**Scene 6 (CTA scene):** Compelling call to action

## Quality Check

Before finalising any script, verify:
- [ ] No full English sentences
- [ ] Hook starts with BM question or statement
- [ ] CTA uses Malaysian expressions (jom, wajib, grab, etc.)
- [ ] Price shown in RM format
- [ ] At least 3 Malaysian slang words used per script
- [ ] Tone is conversational, not like a formal advertisement
- [ ] Product name kept in original form (English product names are fine)
